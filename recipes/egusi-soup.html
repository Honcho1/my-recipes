Egusi Soup Recipe

Description

Egusi Soup is a hearty and creamy dish made with ground melon seeds (egusi), which gives the soup its signature texture and nutty flavor. It is commonly prepared with a variety of leafy vegetables like spinach, ugu, or bitter leaves, and enriched with meats such as beef, goat, or fish. The addition of palm oil, peppers, and other local spices brings out a bold, savory flavor. Egusi Soup is typically enjoyed with pounded yam, fufu, or eba, making it a wholesome and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • Egusi
  • Palm oil
  • Leafy vegetables
  • Meat
  • Stockfish
  • Dry fish
  • Crayfish
  • Pepper
  • Onions
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Salt
  • Water

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix the ground egusi with a little water to form a thick paste.
  2. Heat palm oil in a pot and fry the egusi paste in small lumps, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  3. Add ground crayfish and pepper to the egusi, and fry until golden brown.
  4. Pour in water or stock and allow it to simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Add pre-boiled meats (beef, goat meat, stockfish, or dry fish) and cook for another 10 minutes.
  6. Season with seasoning cubes and salt.
  7. Stir in chopped leafy vegetables (ugu, spinach, or bitter leaves) and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  8. Serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or eba.
