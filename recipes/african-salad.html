Abacha (African Salad) Recipe

Description

Abacha, also known as African Salad, is a traditional delicacy from Eastern Nigeria, particularly popular among the Igbo people. This salad is made from shredded cassava tubers, which are dried and rehydrated before being mixed with palm oil, potash, garden eggs, and a variety of spices. Abacha is often enjoyed as a light meal or snack and can be served with fish, ugba (oil bean), and other proteins. The dish is known for its tangy, savory taste and crunchy texture, making it a refreshing and unique Nigerian meal.

Ingredients

  • Abacha
  • Palm oil
  • Potash
  • Ugba
  • Garden eggs
  • Crayfish
  • Stockfish or dry fish
  • Red onions
  • Ground pepper
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Scent leaves
  • Fresh pepper
  • Kpomo
  • Fried fish

Steps

  1. Soak the dried abacha (shredded cassava) in warm water for 10-15 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, dissolve potash in warm water and strain to remove residues.
  3. Heat palm oil in a pan, then add the potash water to it. Stir until it forms a thick yellowish paste.
  4. Add ground crayfish, pepper, and seasoning cubes to the mixture, and stir well.
  5. Add the soaked abacha into the palm oil mixture and mix thoroughly to combine.
  6. Incorporate ugba (oil bean seeds), sliced onions, garden eggs, and any other protein like fried fish or stockfish.
  7. Garnish with sliced scent leaves and fresh pepper. Adjust seasoning if needed.
  8. Serve chilled or at room temperature, with optional kpomo or fried fish on the side.
