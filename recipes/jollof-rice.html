Jollof Rice Recipe

Description

Jollof Rice is a vibrant and flavorful one-pot dish that has earned its place as a beloved staple across West Africa, particularly in Nigeria. Known for its rich red color, Jollof Rice is made by cooking rice in a spiced tomato sauce infused with onions, garlic, peppers, and a blend of local spices. It is often garnished with vegetables, meat, or fish, and is traditionally served at parties and gatherings. The balance of smoky, spicy, and tangy flavors, combined with its enticing aroma, makes Jollof Rice an irresistible meal.

Ingredients

  • Rice (long grain)
  • Tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • Tomato paste
  • Onions
  • Red bell peppers
  • Scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Thyme
  • Bay leaves
  • Curry powder
  • Vegetable oil
  • Chicken or beef stock
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes

Steps

  1. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet, garlic, and ginger to make a smooth paste.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pot and sauté chopped onions until translucent.
  3. Add tomato paste and fry for about 5 minutes until it darkens.
  4. Add the blended tomato mix and fry until the oil separates from the sauce.
  5. Season with thyme, curry powder, seasoning cubes, and salt. Add bay leaves for extra flavor.
  6. Pour in chicken or beef stock, stir well, and allow it to simmer for about 10 minutes.
  7. Add washed rice to the pot, ensuring it’s well covered by the sauce.
  8. Stir and cover the pot with a tight lid. Reduce heat to low and cook until the rice is tender.
  9. Stir occasionally to prevent burning. If needed, add more stock or water.
  10. Garnish with sliced vegetables or serve with your preferred protein (chicken, beef, or fish).
