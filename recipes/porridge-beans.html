Porridge Beans Recipe

Description

Porridge Beans, also known as "Ewa," is a traditional Nigerian dish made by slowly cooking beans until they are soft and creamy, often infused with palm oil, onions, and a blend of spices. This dish is rich in flavor, with a savory, earthy taste that can be customized with the addition of plantains, yam, or vegetables. Porridge Beans is a nutrient-packed, protein-rich meal, perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, and is often paired with bread, garri, or rice for a complete meal.

Ingredients

  • Brown beans
  • Palm oil
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • Crayfish
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Ripe plantain

Steps

  1. Wash the beans thoroughly and place them in a pot with enough water to cover them.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the beans are soft. Add more water as necessary.
  3. Once the beans are cooked, heat palm oil in a separate pan and saut√© chopped onions and scotch bonnet peppers.
  4. Add ground crayfish and stir for a few minutes, then pour the mixture into the pot of beans.
  5. Season with salt, seasoning cubes, and more pepper if desired. Stir everything together.
  6. Add optional ingredients like plantain or yam cubes and let the beans cook further until creamy and thick.
  7. Serve with bread, garri, or rice.
